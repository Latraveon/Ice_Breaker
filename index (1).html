Why Are Glaciers Melting?

Glaciers and sea ice are melting due to rising global temperatures caused by human-induced climate change. Increased greenhouse gas emissions trap heat in the atmosphere, disrupting the natural balance and causing polar regions to warm at an accelerated rate. The melting ice contributes to rising sea levels and affects ecosystems worldwide.

What We Can Do

  • Reduce carbon emissions by using renewable energy sources like solar and wind power.
  • Minimize the use of single-use plastics to lower pollution.
  • Advocate for policies that combat climate change, such as carbon pricing and renewable energy incentives.
  • Plant trees and preserve forests to absorb carbon dioxide from the atmosphere.
  • Adopt sustainable habits, such as biking, carpooling, and conserving energy at home.
